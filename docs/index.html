Common indicators

A shared analytical framework

The goal is to make national systems comparable while preserving each country’s institutional specificities.

Gross wage Reference wage before employee deductions.
Net wage Wage after employee contributions, before or after tax depending on module.
Employer cost Total monthly cost borne by the employer.
Social wedge Gap between employer cost and net wage.
Employee contributions Mandatory social contributions paid by workers.
Employer contributions Mandatory social contributions paid by employers.
Cost-to-net ratio How much employer cost is needed to deliver one euro of net wage.
Marginal retention Share of an additional euro of gross wage retained by the worker.

Method

Transparent simulations, country by country

Rule-based modelling

Each module is built from explicit institutional parameters: minimum wages, social security rates, contribution ceilings, reductions and tax rules when available.

Open datasets

Country grids are exported as CSV files so that results can be audited, reused and compared across wage levels and profiles.

Versioned parameters

Each module documents the regulatory year, effective date, assumptions and limits of the simulation.

Comparable indicators

The platform standardizes the presentation of net wages, employer costs, contribution rates, wedges and marginal effects.
